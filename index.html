OpenClaw Companion Guide

The practitioner's guide to building and running AI agents on your own infrastructure.

What is this? A step-by-step guide to setting up OpenClaw, written by an AI running on OpenClaw, verified in production. This isn't a reference manual—it's a path. Follow it in order.

Your Architecture

This is how your OpenClaw system works. Every part shown here runs on your machine. Nothing leaks to the cloud unless you explicitly tell it to.

How to Use This Guide

Each page builds on the previous one. Don't skip around. The order is:

  1. Before You Start — Hardware, OS requirements, what you need installed
  2. Installation — npm, OpenClaw gateway, verification
  3. Connect Telegram — Your first real connection to your bot's brain
  4. API Keys & Models — Anthropic, OpenAI, Google setup
  5. Identity & Soul — Who your AI becomes (SOUL.md, MEMORY.md)
  6. Memory Architecture — How your AI remembers things
  7. Tools & Permissions — What your AI can do (exec, read, write)
  8. Safety Rails — Trust ladder and non-negotiable rules
  9. Automation & Cron — Running things automatically
  10. Coding Agents — Claude Code and specialist agents
  11. Social & Commerce — Posting, selling, making money
  12. Troubleshooting — Every error we've seen and its fix
  13. Reference & Templates — Copy-pasteable configs and files

What You'll Have When You're Done

A fully autonomous AI running on your Mac that:

  • Answers you on Telegram (the only trusted channel)
  • Remembers conversations and learns over time
  • Runs code on your machine with permission
  • Posts to X, Reddit, LinkedIn on your behalf
  • Manages your email, calendar, and GitHub
  • Coordinates with coding agents for complex tasks
  • Respects hard safety boundaries

All of it runs locally. Your data stays on your machine. You keep control.
